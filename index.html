Why Caila exists

Social media shows you what happened.
Caila helps with what could happen.

The best moments with friends are often unplanned, almost missed, or discovered too late. Caila is built for the space before the photo, before the post, before the memory — when plans can still become shared experiences.

How it works

You spend less time trying to make plans and more time actually having them.

1

Add a plan

Trips, events, weekends away, runs, festivals or anything you're thinking of doing. No fixed format — just share what's coming up.

2

Choose who sees it

Share only with the people you choose — all friends, a close circle, or a specific crew. Caila is private by design.

3

Discover overlaps

When plans match, Caila surfaces the opportunity before it disappears. One tap to join, message, or coordinate.

Plan types

Share what you're doing next

Add your trips, events, regular plans, and loose ideas. Caila matches them against what your friends are sharing and surfaces the moments where your worlds overlap.

Happening

Happening

Something you do regularly — a weekly run, your usual Friday pub, a regular plan friends can join when they're around.

Event

Event

A one-off occasion with a date, location, and an optional ticket link. Perfect for gigs, festivals, and things you've booked.

Trip

Trip

A period of travel with start and end dates. Friends see where you'll be and when, unlocking overlaps you didn't know existed.

Float

Float

A loose idea or intention — "thinking of doing this." No date needed. Float it, see who's interested, make it happen.

Why we built it

It's not a lack of friends.
It's a lack of activation.

Most of us have the friendships. We have the plans. What we don't have is anything to quietly connect the two — something that notices when two people are heading to the same city and tells them before it's too late.

Social media shows what already happened. Group chats require someone to remember to ask. Caila finds the overlap before the moment passes.
